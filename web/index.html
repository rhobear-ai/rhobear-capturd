Help & best practices

Get a great video on the first try.

One flow films best. "Sign up and create a project" beats "show everything." If you have two stories, run two captures. Short, focused videos hold attention and let the camera actually zoom in on what matters.

Give the URL where the action starts (your app, a signup page, a specific feature). The homepage is a poster, not a walkthrough. If your app is behind a login, use demo mode so you can drive past the gate.

The camera punches in on whatever you click, hardest on small chips and icons. Tell it "click the plan picker" rather than "scroll around," and every step lands a tight, readable close-up instead of a vague pan.

Hit the mic and talk it through: "click the house button, now type this." It performs each step on camera while you speak, and nothing you say shows up in the recording. Perfect for logins, payments, and anything the agent cannot reach on its own.

The best product clips are short. Four to eight steps keeps the pacing snappy so a viewer can catch the whole thing in a scroll. If a flow is longer, split it or trim to the moments that sell it.

Point it at a public URL and it films what is live. Connect the repo and it can render your exact build, so the video matches the version you are about to ship, not whatever is cached in production.
